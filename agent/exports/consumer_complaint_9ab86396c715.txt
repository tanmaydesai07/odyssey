### SUBJECT: Complaint under Section 35 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 seeking Redressal for Supply of Defective Mobile Phone

---

## MOST RESPECTFULLY SHOWETH:

**1.** That the Complainant is a consumer as defined under Section 2(7) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"), having purchased a mobile phone from the Opposite Party for a total consideration of ₹25,000.00 (Rupees Twenty-Five Thousand Only).

**2.** That the Complainant purchased the said mobile phone on [Date of Purchase] from the retail outlet/online platform of the Opposite Party situated at [Location of Purchase].

**3.** That the Complainant made full payment of ₹25,000.00 towards the purchase of the mobile phone and was issued a valid bill/receipt bearing Bill No. ______ dated ______.

**4.** That the mobile phone so purchased was found to be defective within the warranty period. The defect(s) observed include: (a) [Describe defect - e.g., screen display malfunction, battery drainage issue, device heating, speaker not working, etc.]; (b) [Additional defect, if any]; and (c) [Any other defect].

**5.** That the aforementioned defect(s) were noticed by the Complainant within [Number] days of purchase, thereby making the product unfit for the purpose for which it was purchased, as the phone does not function in accordance with the expected standards.

**6.** That the Opposite Party is engaged in the business of selling electronic goods, including mobile phones, and is bound to ensure that the goods supplied are free from defects and conform to the standards as prescribed under the Act.

**7.** That the Complainant approached the Opposite Party on [Date of First Complaint] to report the defect and requested either: (a) repair of the mobile phone; or (b) replacement with a new unit; or (c) full refund of the purchase price.

**8.** That despite clear intimation and multiple follow-ups by the Complainant, the Opposite Party failed to redress the grievance and neglected its duty to provide a satisfactory resolution, thereby committing deficiency in service as defined under Section 2(11) of the Act.

**9.** That the Complainant has been put to mental agony, harassment, and financial loss due to the defective product and the negligent attitude of the Opposite Party in not addressing the complaint.

**10.** That the Opposite Party has committed unfair trade practice by selling a defective product and by failing to honour its statutory obligations under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.

---

## LEGAL GROUNDS

**(i)** That the Opposite Party has committed deficiency in service as contemplated under Section 2(11) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, by supplying a defective mobile phone.

**(ii)** That the Opposite Party has violated the provisions of Sections 2(47) and 2(51) of the Act relating to unfair trade practices.

**(iii)** That the Opposite Party has failed to comply with its obligation to provide effective redressal as mandated under Section 35 of the Act.

**(iv)** That the Complainant being a consumer is entitled to relief under Section 38 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.

**(v)** That the cause of action arose at the place where the purchase was made, and the same continues within the jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Commission.

---

## RELIEF SOUGHT

The Complainant humbly prays that this Hon'ble Commission may be pleased to:

1. **Direct the Opposite Party** to refund the entire sum of ₹25,000.00 (Rupees Twenty-Five Thousand Only) paid by the Complainant towards the purchase of the defective mobile phone; OR

2. **In the alternative**, direct the Opposite Party to replace the defective mobile phone with a brand new, functioning unit of the same model/specification; AND

3. **Direct the Opposite Party** to pay compensation of ₹[Amount] towards mental agony, harassment, and Litigation costs; AND

4. **Grant any other relief** as this Hon'ble Commission deems fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case.
