## FACTS OF THE COMPLAINT

The Complainant most respectfully submits and states before this Honourable Commission as follows:

1. That the Complainant is a consumer within the meaning of Section 2(7) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") and is a law-abiding citizen of India, ordinarily residing at the above-stated address. The Opposite Party is a "seller" and "e-commerce entity" within the meanings of Sections 2(37) and 2(16) of the Act, respectively, engaged in the business of operating an online marketplace/platform through its mobile application and website, www.flipkart.com, for the sale of various consumer goods to the general public.

2. That on or about the 10th day of March 2025, the Complainant, acting in good faith and relying upon the representations made by the Opposite Party through its platform, placed an order on the Flipkart mobile application to purchase one (1) Samsung Galaxy M34 5G smartphone (hereinafter referred to as "the Product") for a total consideration of **Rs. 18,499** (Rupees Eighteen Thousand Four Hundred and Ninety-Nine only). The order was assigned Order ID: **OD329847561234**. A true and genuine copy of the order confirmation email dated 10 March 2025 is annexed hereto and marked as **Annexure — A**.

3. That the Complainant made full and complete payment of the said sum of Rs. 18,499 through online payment gateway facilitated by the Opposite Party. The payment was successfully processed and received by the Opposite Party, as evidenced in the order confirmation and payment receipt available on the Flipkart platform.

4. That the Product was delivered by the Opposite Party through its logistics/delivery partner on or about the evening of 11 March 2025 at the Complainant's residential address stated above. The delivery was made in what appeared to be a sealed and intact flipkart packet/parcel.

5. That upon receipt of the packet, the Complainant proceeded to open the sealed box of the Product in the presence of his family members. A video recording of the unboxing was made by the Complainant, which clearly shows that **instead of the Samsung Galaxy M34 5G smartphone, the sealed box contained a ordinary commercial soap bar ( toilet soap)**. The box, despite appearing externally sealed, had been tampered with, and the original phone had been deliberately removed and replaced with an inanimate object. True and genuine photographs of the delivery packet and the incorrect contents (the soap bar) are annexed hereto and marked as **Annexure — B**.

6. That the Complainant was shell-shocked and greatly disturbed to discover that for the price of a high-end smartphone (Rs. 18,499), he had been delivered a cheap soap bar. This constitutes not merely a deficiency in service but a fraudulent act, demonstrating a serious breach of trust and a blatant unfair trade practice on the part of the Opposite Party. The Opposite Party, being a large and reputed e-commerce entity, failed to ensure the integrity and sanctity of the products sold and delivered through its platform.

7. That on the very same day, i.e., on 11 March 2025, the Complainant raised a return request through the Flipkart application, seeking return of the Product and a full refund of the amount paid. However, the Opposite Party, without any genuine or proper investigation, summarily rejected the return request on or about 12 March 2025, citing a frivolous and untenable reason — *"product not eligible for return."* A true and genuine screenshot of the rejection message is annexed hereto and marked as **Annexure — C**.

8. That the Complainant, being a diligent consumer, made consistent and bonafide efforts to resolve the matter at the level of the Opposite Party before approaching this Honourable Commission. The Complainant made as many as three (3) telephone calls to the Flipkart customer care helpline on separate dates, specifically on or about 12 March 2025, 13 March 2025, and 14 March 2025, seeking a replacement of the Product or a full refund. Despite acknowledging the seriousness of the complaint, the customer care representatives failed to provide any satisfactory resolution and/or failed to escalate the matter to the appropriate grievance resolution authority. True and genuine call records are annexed hereto and marked as **Annexure — D**.

9. That further, on the 15th day of March 2025, the Complainant sent a detailed email to the grievance redressal officer of the Opposite Party at the designated email id, explaining the entire sequence of events and demanding immediate remedial action, including a full refund. However, to the Complainant's shock and distress, the Opposite Party failed to respond to the email even within the statutory timeline prescribed under Section 35(4) of the Act. The email sent by the Complainant is annexed hereto and marked as **Annexure — E**.

10. That the acts and omissions of the Opposite Party, as detailed in the foregoing paragraphs, constitute a **"deficiency"** within the meaning of Section 2(11) of the Act, as the Opposite Party failed to provide the service to the extent required of it under the circumstances, and failed to deliver the correct product as ordered and paid for. Further, the Opposite Party has engaged in an **"unfair trade practice"** within the meaning of Section 2(47) of the Act, by delivering a product entirely different from the one ordered and paid for, thereby cheating and defrauding the Complainant. Such conduct also amounts to a violation of the statutory duty cast upon the seller/e-commerce entity under the Act.

11. That as a direct consequence of the fraudulent delivery and the arbitrary rejection of the return request, the Complainant has suffered significant **mental agony, harassment, and distress**. The Complainant, a law-abiding consumer who placed a genuine order and made full payment in trust, was subjected to absolute non-cooperation and indifference by the Opposite Party. The Complainant has also incurred financial loss to the extent of Rs. 18,499.

12. That there exists a genuine consumer dispute between the Complainant and the Opposite Party, which entitles the Complainant to the relief sought herein, and this Honourable Commission has the jurisdiction to entertain and adjudicate upon the present complaint under Section 35 and other relevant provisions of the Act.

## LEGAL GROUNDS

The present Complaint is filed on, inter alia, the following legal grounds:

1. **Section 2(7) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019** — The Complainant is a "consumer" as he purchased goods for a consideration paid or payable, for the purpose of satisfying his personal need and not for resale or commercial purposes.

2. **Section 2(11) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019** — The Opposite Party is guilty of "deficiency" in service, as it failed to deliver the product ordered and paid for by the Complainant. The delivery of an entirely different article (a soap bar) constitutes a fundamental failure to perform the service in accordance with the terms of the contract.

3. **Section 2(47) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019** — The Opposite Party has engaged in an "unfair trade practice" by delivering a misbranded/defective product and arbitrarily rejecting the Complainant's legitimate return request, thereby cheating the consumer.

4. **Section 35 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019** — This Honourable Commission has the jurisdiction to entertain and adjudicate upon a complaint relating to any dispute between a consumer and a seller, where the value of the goods or services claimed does not exceed Rs. 1 Crore. The present complaint well falls within the pecuniary jurisdiction of this Commission.

5. **Section 36 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019** — This Honourable Commission has the power to grant relief as claimed herein, including relief by way of refund, replacement, and compensation.

6. The Opposite Party is further liable under the general principles of law governing contracts and under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, for having failed to ensure the correctness and integrity of the products sold and delivered
